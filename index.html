Access to Justice

Legal Representation in Georgia

This map - a collaboration between Chad Wigington and Joshua Weitz at Georgia Tech and the Center for Access to Justice at Georgia State University College of Law - provides information regarding access to justice in the state of Georgia. Each county is shaded according to the metric chosen at the bottom left.

About

In the top right, counties are listed alphabetically and can be selected to show their location.

In the top right, map styles can be changed for a better user experience.

Selecting a metric from the drop-down menu on the bottom left changes the map coloring scheme.

Hovering the mouse within the borders of a county will display the county's information in the bottom right corner. Likewise, the color of the county will match the color of the previously selected metric.

About the data

The State Bar of Georgia provided the number of lawyers by county. By combining this data with available census data, statistics from the Federal Communications Commission and the Center for Neighborhood Technology (http://alltransit.cnt.org/), and information from Georgia Legal Services Program and the Atlanta Legal Aid Society, the map provides insight into attorney representation and other factors that impact access to justice throughout the state.


Map designed by Charles Wigington, March 2017.
